Key Findings & Recommendations

1. Satisfaction drives retention. Customers with CSAT ≤ 2 churn at dramatically higher rates than those rating 4–5 — statistically significant (p < 0.001).
2. Email engagement is an early-warning signal. At-risk customers show measurably lower email open rates well before they churn — automate win-back triggers when open rate drops below 10%.
3. Champions carry disproportionate value. The "Champion" segment shows the highest CLV scores despite being a minority of the customer base — protecting this segment should be priority #1.
4. K-Means reveals 4 natural customer archetypes beyond the manually-defined segments, validated via PCA — useful for refining future targeting models.
5. Mobile dominates traffic, but Desktop converts higher value. Desktop users show higher average order values — worth a checkout-flow investment.
6. Sports & Fitness and Electronics drive disproportionate revenue — concentrate inventory, promotions, and influencer partnerships here.
